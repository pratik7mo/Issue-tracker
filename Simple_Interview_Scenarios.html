Interview Scenarios: ELI5 Edition

Issue Tracking System | Role Explanations for Everyone
Q1: Why couldn't we see the "boxes" in Jenkins initially?
Technical Fix: Refactored from a script with logic gaps into a strictly defined Declarative Pipeline. This allows the Jenkins UI (Blue Ocean) to correctly map steps to visualizations.
ELI5: "It's like a robot trying to cook a meal without a recipe on the screen. I updated the instructions so now the robot (Jenkins) shows us a clear digital checklist as it cooks every part of our app."
Q2: What is Docker's role in this project?
Technical Fix: Implemented containerization to bundle the application with its dependencies (JVM, libraries) into a portable image.
ELI5: "Docker is like a shipping container. We pack our software into it so it stays safe and works perfectly, whether it's on a truck, a ship, or an airplane (different computers)."
Q3: How do you handle production security?
Technical Fix: Used Jenkins credentials and withCredentials to inject secrets into a .env file at runtime.
ELI5: "Instead of writing the door-password in a book everyone can see, the robot (Jenkins) only whispers it to the app right before it opens the door to the public."
Q4: Why use Nginx as a reverse proxy?
Technical Fix: Configured Nginx to proxy /api requests internally, bypassing CORS issues and simplifying client-side routing.
ELI5: "Nginx is like a receptionist. Instead of you having to search for every office in the building, you just tell the receptionist what you need, and they handle the directions for you."
